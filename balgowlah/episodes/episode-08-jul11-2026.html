One swing at the 17th

Graeme Smith stood on the 17th, a par 3, and did the thing every golfer has rehearsed in their head a thousand times. One swing, and the next thing anyone knew the ball was in the hole. A hole-in-one, confirmed, and drinks presumably accounted for.

It carried him to 4th on 37 points, his first top-10 of the season. Some Saturdays the game takes. This one, for Graeme, gave.

Winner Story
The only 40 on the property

Avery Ellis posted 40 points off 33, a full 10.6 clear of a field average of 29.4, and still only had one to spare over Phil Moreton's 39. That is the top of this board in a sentence.

It wasn't spotless either. A 7 at the 4th was worth a single point and there was a pickup on the card. Ellis just out-scored the damage, took 4 points at the 14th, and came home in 21 after going out in 19.

Villain Hole
You have to play the 6th twice
The card calls them the 6th and the 15th, but it's the same argument twice, and the course won it both times. The 15th was the hardest hole on the property at 1.27 points a man with 21 wipes, and the 6th ran second at 1.31 with 21 wipes of its own. The 3rd did its usual quiet work with 18 wipes, and this week that was only good for third.

Form Watch
The bankers
Stu McPhail has now made eight cuts in a row, the longest run going, and did it this week with the steadiest card in the field, 19 out and 19 back for 3rd on 38.
Andrew Revill, the Episode 6 winner, backed it up with 6th on 37. The win is starting to look less like a visit and more like a residence.
Sharpshooter
Three over the card
Strip the handicaps away and Mark Bradley owned the golf. Off a mark of 3, he went round 3 over the card, the sharpest ball-striking of the day, worth 36 points and 13th.
